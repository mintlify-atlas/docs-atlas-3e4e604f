This guide walks you through creating a working key map from scratch. By the end you will have remapped a physical button to do something new.

## Before you start

Key Mapper requires the accessibility service to be running. Without it, the app cannot detect button presses.

<Warning>
  The accessibility service must stay enabled for your key maps to work. Some Android manufacturers (Samsung, Xiaomi, Huawei, and others) aggressively kill background services to save battery. If your key maps stop working after a while, check the [troubleshooting guide](/guides/troubleshooting).
</Warning>

## Enable the accessibility service

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Key Mapper">
    Launch Key Mapper from your app drawer. The home screen shows your list of key maps (empty on first launch) and a status bar at the top.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap the accessibility service prompt">
    If the service is not running, the home screen shows a red alert banner. Tap **Enable** on the banner, or go to **Settings** (the gear icon in the top bar) and tap the accessibility service option.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable the service in Android settings">
    Android opens the **Accessibility** settings screen. Find **Key Mapper** in the list and tap it. On the next screen, toggle it on and confirm when Android warns you about the accessibility service permissions.

    <Note>
      On Android 13 and later, some manufacturers mark Key Mapper as a "restricted setting". If you see this warning, tap **Allow restricted settings** before you can enable the toggle.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Return to Key Mapper">
    Press the back button or navigate back to Key Mapper. The red banner should be gone, replaced by a green indicator showing the service is running.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Create your first key map

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tap the + button">
    On the Key Mapper home screen, tap the **+** floating action button in the bottom-right corner. This opens the key map editor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Record a trigger">
    In the **Trigger** section, tap **Record trigger**. Key Mapper now listens for button presses.

    Press the physical button you want to remap — for example, the **volume down** button. The button appears in the trigger list. Tap **Stop recording** when you are done.

    <Tip>
      You can add multiple keys to a trigger. Keys pressed together form a parallel trigger; keys pressed one after another form a sequence trigger. For your first key map, use a single key.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a click type">
    Below the recorded key you can set whether the trigger fires on a **short press**, **long press**, or **double press**. Leave it on **Short press** for now.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add an action">
    Scroll down to the **Actions** section and tap **Add action**. A picker opens with categories of available actions.

    For a first example, choose **Key event** and pick **KEYCODE_MEDIA_NEXT** (next track). Tap **Done** to confirm.

    <Tip>
      You can add more than one action. Each action in the list fires in order when the trigger activates. Tap the delay icon between actions to add a pause.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the key map">
    Tap the **Save** button (tick icon) in the top-right corner. Your key map now appears on the home screen with a green enabled indicator.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Test your key map

Press the button you recorded. The action should fire immediately. If nothing happens:

- Check that the Key Mapper service is still running (green indicator on the home screen)
- Make sure the key map is enabled (the toggle next to it should be on)
- Check that there are no constraint errors shown on the key map card

## Common first key map examples

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Volume down → skip to next track">
    - **Trigger**: Volume down, long press
    - **Action**: Key event → `KEYCODE_MEDIA_NEXT`

    Long-pressing volume down skips to the next song while a short press still adjusts volume normally (you can keep the original behaviour by not consuming the key event).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Volume up + volume down → pause or play media">
    - **Trigger**: Volume up + Volume down, parallel, short press
    - **Action**: Key event → `KEYCODE_MEDIA_PLAY_PAUSE`

    Pressing both volume buttons at the same time toggles media playback.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Double press volume up → toggle torch">
    - **Trigger**: Volume up, double press
    - **Action**: Toggle flashlight

    Double-tapping the volume up button turns the flashlight on or off.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Gamepad button → open an app">
    - **Trigger**: Record the desired gamepad button
    - **Action**: App → choose your app from the list

    Connect a gamepad over Bluetooth or USB, record one of its buttons, and assign it to launch any installed app.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
